About me

My interests of research are focused in two dissimilar subjects.

  1. I am interested in the emerging interactions between optimization and geometry.
  2. I am interested in the conception of actuarial models specifically looking for new mathematical methods for the study of pension funds.

The first is my main research subject, I started to work designing mathematical models employing in several cases optimization methods; I was focused to only employ well know results to the estimation complex statistical models, however my interest changed from applications to theoretical results and fundamental consequences impacting another areas in Mathematics, in particular I try to study and comprehend geometrical interpretations of optimization methods and the computation and design of new algebraic invariants related to enumerative problems in geometry.

Time ago, I decided to bifurcate the path of my career as mathematician; as consequence my second research interest emerged, during this time I also gained a good and quite unique experience working for Risko S.A. a risk management enterprise. In these years, I concentrated a good part of my energy to research, conceive, design, integrate and implement several mathematical models with actuarial applications. In particular, jointly with the actuarial team of Risko S.A. we have implemented several valuation models for pension funds, health insurance and other products inside the insurance industry.

My work experience as mathematician has been related to the implementation of mathematical models, for additional details you can check my github profile where I decided to version several projects with different purposes, some of then finished, others in working progress.
