About me

My main interests of research are focused in two dissimilar subjects.

  1. I am interested in the emerging interactions between optimization and geometry where several methods are related to combinatorics, algebra and theory of algorithms.
  2. I am interested in the generation of actuarial models with better prediction, specifically looking for new mathematical methods for the study of pension funds.

The first is my main research subject, where I am mostly interested in the theoretical results and fundamental consequences impacting another research areas in mathematics. I started to work in Optimization, as a final user, only employing the well know results to the estimation complex statistical models, however my interest growths in interest from the applications to theoretical results and impacting others areas of mathematics, in particular the geometrical interpretations and consequences and the discovery and computation of new algebraic invariants.

Time ago, I decided to bifurcate the path of my career as mathematician; as consequence my second research interest emerged, during this time I gained a good and quite unique experience working for Risko S.A. a risk management enterprise. In these years, I concentrated a good part of my energy to research, conceive, design, integrate and implement several mathematical models with actuarial applications. In particular, jointly with the actuarial team of Risko S.A. we have implemented several valuation models for pension funds and other insurance products inside the insurance industry.

My work experience as mathematician has been always related to the implementation of mathematical models, you can check my github profile where I decided to version several projects with different purposes, some of then finished, others in working progress.
